About Me

From the plant floor
to the data pipeline

I've spent years in manufacturing, and somewhere along the way data became the most interesting part of the job. Working my way into a Continuous Improvement Operations Specialist role taught me that data is most valuable when it's actually being used to uncover something — not just report on it.

I originally started my Associate's in Computer Programming about ten years ago but stepped away when life required it. I'm back now to finish what I started, with a much clearer picture of where I want to go and real-world experience to back it up.

The turning point came when I facilitated an MES rollout at our facility. Watching what was possible when that data was actually leveraged flipped a switch. Since then I've been focused on connecting systems, automating manual reporting, and building tools that give leadership visibility they didn't have before.

I've always believed that focusing on the process over the result is what makes success repeatable — and that's the approach I use when solving problems.
